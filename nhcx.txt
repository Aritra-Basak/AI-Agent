National Health Claims Exchange
The National Health Claims Exchange (NHCX) is developed under the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) by the National Health Authority (NHA) in consultation with Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI). With the primary purpose to streamline and standardize the processing of health insurance claims across the country, leveraging the Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) standards to ensure interoperability across diverse systems. NHCX Specification is a standardized communication protocol designed to facilitate the seamless exchange of health claim information among payers, providers, beneficiaries, and other relevant entities.

By design it ensures interoperability across diverse systems, enabling machine-readable, auditable, and verifiable exchanges of information. By adhering to open standards, it ensures that the data shared is accurate, reliable, and trustworthy, enhancing the efficiency and transparency of health claim processes. This robust framework supports the integration of various stakeholders within the healthcare ecosystem, promoting a more efficient and cohesive exchange of critical health claim data.
